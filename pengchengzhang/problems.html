It seems that there have been many conjectures or open problems in the field of modular forms, but it is often hard for young researches like myself to get to know about them. I have thus compiled this list, which is only restricted to open problems (that I am aware of) regarding the coefficients of modular forms, in hopes of spreading them to other young researchers in this field. Please feel free to let me know if you spot any mistakes, want to add more to the list, or are aware of existing solutions to any of them.

Maeda's conjecture first appeared as Conjecture 1.2 in a paper by Hida and Maeda. The conjecture in its stronger version is nowadays usually formulated as the following.

Conjecture 1. For all positive integers \(n\) and all weights \(k\) such that \(S_k(1)\) is nonempty,

  • the characteristic polynomial \(\phi_p\) of the Hecke operator \(T_p\) on \(S_k(1)\) is irreducible over \(\mathbb{Q}\), and
  • the Galois group of the splitting field of \(\phi_p\) over \(\mathbb{Q}\) is the symmetric group \(S_d\), where \(d\) is the dimension of \(S_k(1)\).

A weaker version of the conjecture is the following, which follows from the stronger version.

Conjecture 2. For all weights \(k\), the Galois action on the set of Hecke eigenforms in \(S_k(1)\) has a single orbit.

There have been proposed generalizations of this conjecture to the case of higher level. See e.g. the paper by Tsaknias. In particular, the paper makes the following conjecture for squarefree level (Conjecture 2.4 in the paper).

Conjecture 3. Let \(N\) be a product of \(s\) distinct prime numbers. For \(k\) sufficiently large, the Galois action on the set of newforms in \(S^\mathrm{new}_k(\Gamma_0(N))\) has exactly \(2^s\) orbits.

If we write \(N=p_1p_2\cdots p_s\), then the space \(S^\mathrm{new}_k(\Gamma_0(N))\) decomposes into a direct sum of \(2^s\) Galois stable subspaces according to the Artin–Lehner sign at each \(p_i\), so this conjecture is essentially saying that the Galois action on the set of newforms in each of these subspaces has a single orbit when the weight is sufficiently large.

The abstract of a paper by Pa≈üol and Zudilin describes the following (folklore) conjecture.

Conjecture 1. There does not exist a holomorphic modular form \(f=\sum_{n=1}^\infty a_nq^n\) with integer coefficients such that \(n\) divides \(a_n\) for all \(n\).

If we lose the holomorphic condition, then there in fact exist meromorphic modular forms, e.g. \(\Delta/E_4^2\), satisfying the divisibility property stated in the conjecture. The divisibility property of \(\Delta/E_4^2\) was (more or less) proved in the paper by Li and Neururer.
